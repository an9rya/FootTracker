Food Waste:

An average Canadian household generates 140 kg of food waste per year. This is equivalent to 56.6 tonnes of CO2

However, 60% of this is avoidable through sustainable practices such as donating, proper storage, and avoiding overbuying.

In Canada, 4.82 million tonnes of food are lost in processing and manufacturing.


Plastic Waste:

The average Canadian will produce about 105 kg of plastic waste each year, 37% coming from packaging.

A majority of Canadians already practice sustainable methods to reduce plastic waste such as reusable water bottles and bags.


Electric Waste:

In 2019, the annual electricity consumption per Canadian household was 15 megawatt-hours.

Fortunately, 81% of Canadian households employ some form of energy-saving lighting.


Transportation Waste:

The average vehicle mileage per year in Canada is 15200 km and the average distance travelled by a passenger is 2182 km by plane.

In just September 2022, 120.6 million trips were taken in Canadian transit, saving 2.2 tonnes per person overall.

Only roughly a third of employed Canadians use sustainable methods of transportation.

In 2021, 5.3% of registered Canadian vehicles were electric or hybrid.
